10

Principles
for
Good Design

1

Good design is innovative

The possibilities for innovation are not, by any means, exhausted. Technological development is always offering new opportunities for innovative design. But innovative design always develops in tandem with innovative technology, and can never be an end in itself.

2

Good design is intentional

Every part of a design needs to be done with purpose and intention. This helps strengthen the overall idea of a piece.

3

Good design is aesthetic

The aesthetic quality of a product is integral to its usefulness because products we use every day affect our person and our well-being. But only well-executed objects can be beautiful.

4

Good design is understandable

Every piece of design has an idea behind it. A designer should be able to convey that idea so the audience can understand it.

5

Good design is thorough

Rather than having one element or color to make the design stand out, it should be through and thought out so many pieces can be combined to work together.

6

Good design is honest

It does not make a product more innovative, powerful or valuable than it really is. It does not attempt to manipulate the consumer with promises that cannot be kept.

7

Good design is balanced

Font, color, and lines all play into this idea of balance. This idea helps soften things for the eye and make the whole piece easier to view.

8

Good design uses contrast

It does not make a product more innovative, powerful or valuable than it really is. It does not attempt to manipulate the consumer with promises that cannot be kept.

9

Good design manipulates space

Space can completely transform a design. By manipulating space a designer can guide the viewer's eyes around a design.

10

Good design is united

Many separate elements are able to work together as one and convey a message.
